Approach

Recovery you can point to on a chart.

Every plan starts with a baseline and ends with a number you can see improve.

Dr. Tooba, DPT, MS, NMPT
The Physio Studio

I trained as a physical therapist because I wanted rehab to feel less like guesswork and more like engineering — a clear baseline, a clear target, and a plan to close the gap.

Since starting practice, I've worked with runners returning from stress fractures, weekend athletes rebuilding a shoulder after surgery, and people simply tired of living around their own back pain. The tools are the same each time: careful assessment, hands-on treatment, and a home program that actually fits a real week.

Sessions run 45–60 minutes, one-on-one, with the same clinician throughout your plan — no rotating staff, no repeating your history at every visit.

  • Doctor of Physical Therapy (DPT)
  • Master of Science (MS)
  • Neuro-Musculoskeletal Physical Therapist (NMPT)
  • Manual therapy & therapeutic exercise

Process

Four visits. One continuous plan.

The same sequence runs through every course of treatment, adjusted to your goals.

01

Assess

A full evaluation of strength, range of motion, and movement patterns to find the real source of the problem.

02

Treat

Hands-on manual therapy paired with targeted exercise, done together in-session.

03

Track

Every visit is measured — range, strength, and pain scores — so progress is visible, not assumed.

04

Progress

Your home program advances as your numbers improve, until you're back to full function.
